Understanding hearing loss

Plain-language information on the signs, types, and clinical implications of hearing loss — for patients and the family members who care for them.

Why patients visit

Six common reasons patients walk in

Drawn from real reasons our patients have shared in their reviews — these are the most frequent presentations we see at the clinic.

1

A blocked or muffled ear

Often sudden, sometimes after using cotton buds. Usually resolved in a single 20-minute earwax removal appointment.

2

Hearing has dropped in one ear

Asymmetric hearing change is a common presenting symptom — frequently caused by wax impaction, occasionally by something requiring further investigation.

3

Wanting a professional check

You suspect your hearing may have changed but aren't sure. A diagnostic hearing test gives you a clear, printed answer.

4

Considering hearing aids

You've been thinking about it but want to understand the options before committing. The Hearing Aids Evaluation is refundable on purchase.

5

Persistent ringing or buzzing

Tinnitus that won't quiet down — particularly noticeable in quiet rooms. Tinnitus management strategies can reduce its impact.

6

Aftercare for existing devices

Reprogramming, fine-tuning, or troubleshooting for hearing aids purchased elsewhere — supports Signia, Phonak, Starkey, and ReSound.

Types of hearing loss

Three categories — and why it matters which one you have

Most common

Sensorineural

Damage to the inner ear or auditory nerve. Usually permanent. Caused by ageing, noise exposure, genetics, or some medications.

  • Treated with hearing aids or cochlear implants
  • Often gradual and bilateral
  • Most common in adults over 60
Often reversible

Conductive

Sound can't reach the inner ear due to a blockage or middle-ear issue. Often treatable — sometimes resolved in a single visit.

  • Caused by earwax, fluid, or infection
  • Can also be due to bone or eardrum issues
  • Treated with cleaning, medication, or sometimes surgery
Combined

Mixed

A combination of sensorineural and conductive components. Treatment depends on which is dominant.

  • Often needs both medical and amplification approach
  • We'll identify both during a full hearing assessment
  • Common in older adults with multiple ear issues
Why it matters

The health risks of untreated hearing loss

Hearing loss isn't just a quality-of-life issue. Recent clinical research has linked it to some of the most serious health risks facing older adults.

2–5×
Higher dementia risk
Mid-life hearing loss is the largest modifiable risk factor for dementia, accounting for an estimated 8% of all cases.
— Lancet Commission, 2020
3×
Higher risk of falls
Even a mild 25-decibel loss triples the risk of a fall — every additional 10dB nearly doubles it again.
— Johns Hopkins Medicine
+50%
Higher depression risk
Adults with untreated hearing loss are 50% more likely to experience clinical depression and social withdrawal.
— National Council on Aging

Frequently asked questions

The questions we're asked most often, by patients and caregivers. Can't find yours? WhatsApp us — a real audiologist will reply.

Do I need a referral to book a hearing test?
No referral is required. You can book directly with us by form, WhatsApp, or phone. Same-week appointments are usually available.
How long does a hearing test take?
A standard diagnostic hearing assessment takes 45–60 minutes including the consultation, otoscopy, audiometry, and a full results discussion. You'll leave with a printed report you can share with your GP.
Is the test painful or invasive?
Not at all. You'll listen to a series of soft tones through headphones in a sound-treated room and respond when you hear them. The hardest part is sitting still.
Do you take walk-ins?
We strongly prefer appointments so we can give you our full attention. WhatsApp us and we'll usually fit you in the same week — often the same day for earwax removal.
Which hearing aid brands do you fit?
We work with three trusted partner brands — Signia, ReSound GN, and Phonak — selected for the latest technology, performance, and ongoing support. Recommendations are made based on your hearing assessment and lifestyle requirements.
What does the Hearing Aids Evaluation involve?
A 1.5-hour pre-fitting consultation that reviews your hearing assessment, listening environments, and lifestyle. The S$100 fee is refundable if hearing aids are purchased within 30 days of evaluation.
Do you offer Real Ear Measurement?
Yes — Real Ear Measurement is the gold standard in hearing aid fitting verification, and is included as standard with every hearing aid fitting at The Hearing Room.
Can you reprogram hearing aids purchased elsewhere?
Yes. Our reprogramming service supports Signia, Phonak, Starkey, and ReSound devices purchased from other practices. The 1-hour service is S$300.
How long does it take to adjust to hearing aids?
Most patients adjust within 2–4 weeks of consistent daily wear. We provide constant support and regular follow-ups to ensure your hearing aids are optimised throughout the adjustment period.
Is earwax removal safe?
Yes. The procedure is performed by a trained clinician under direct otoscopic visualisation, using a safe, non-invasive irrigation technique. It is significantly safer than self-cleaning with cotton buds.
Will it hurt?
No. The procedure is painless, and most appointments are completed in 20 minutes.
How often should I have my ears cleaned?
For most healthy ears, only when symptoms appear — for example, a blocked feeling, hearing loss, or tinnitus. Hearing aid wearers usually benefit from a clean every 6 to 12 months as preventive maintenance.
Can I use cotton buds at home?
We do not recommend it. Cotton buds tend to push wax further into the ear canal and can damage the eardrum. The ear is naturally self-cleaning — if wax is causing symptoms, please come in for an assessment.
What does the S$100 fee cover?
The S$100 earwax removal fee includes the S$25 otoscopy and consultation fee, the procedure itself, and a post-procedure review. The full appointment is approximately 20 minutes.
Why do you charge for hearing services?
Our published fees reflect the time, training, and clinical equipment involved in each appointment. They allow each consultation to remain a comprehensive clinical service, with the audiologist's full attention and a printed report regardless of outcome.
Is the Hearing Aids Evaluation refundable?
Yes — the S$100 evaluation fee is fully refundable if hearing aids are subsequently purchased within 30 days of the evaluation.
Do you accept Medisave or insurance?
Hearing aids are not currently Medisave-claimable in Singapore for most patients. Some private insurance plans may cover diagnostic services — we can provide an itemised receipt for claims. We recommend checking with your insurer first.
Are payment plans available for hearing aids?
Yes. We offer 0% interest instalment plans through major credit cards. Please ask us during your fitting consultation.
Where exactly are you located?
1 Coleman Street, #03-21 The Adelphi, Singapore 179803. Two minutes' walk from City Hall MRT (Exit B or D).
Is there parking?
Yes — basement parking at The Adelphi. Take the lift to L3 and we're at unit #03-21.
Is the clinic wheelchair-accessible?
Yes. Lift access from the basement carpark and ground floor. The consultation rooms are designed for wheelchair access.
Can a family member sit in with me?
Absolutely — we encourage it. Two sets of ears in the consultation room helps you remember everything we discuss.
